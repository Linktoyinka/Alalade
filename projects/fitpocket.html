The Brief

About FitPocket

FitPocket is an educational platform designed to empower users to improve their financial literacy, make informed financial decisions, and ultimately achieve financial independence.

Problem

Financial literacy is often not included in the education systems worldwide, leaving many young adults without the necessary knowledge and skills to make informed financial decisions, leading to financial difficulties.

Objective

Design a cross-platform app that will provide accessible and interactive tools to educate users on key financial concepts and help them improve their financial literacy.

The Brief

About FitPocket

I conducted a survey of 30 participants to gather information about their needs, current financial literacy knowledge and their learning preferences.

About FitPocket

70%

70% of the surveyed participants reported a lack of understanding of basic financial literacy concepts, such as budgeting and saving.

60%

The preferred method of learning for most participants (60%) was through interactive and engaging content, such as games and quizzes.

30%

Only about 30% of respondents have used a financial literacy app or service before. Respondents appreciated the ability to track their spending and set budgets but reported dissatisfaction with the lack of personalized advice and guidance.

Interviews

I interviewed 5 participants representative of the target demography to gather in-depth information about their learning experiences and challenges with financial literacy.

Some of the questions asked during the interview include:
Can you tell me about your current understanding of financial literacy concepts?
Have you used any financial literacy resources in the past? If so, what were your experiences with them?
What are the biggest challenges you face when it comes to financial literacy?

Personas

Based on the information gathered from the research exercises, I created 2 user personas that are representative of the target demography.

Jake Adams.

Occupation
Research Assistant
Location
New York, USA
Age
32
Family
Married (no children)

Problem Statement

Jake is a working student who needs to improve his financial literacy, because he wants a financially comfortable life for his family

Biography

Jake is a postgraduate student running his Doctorate program and working as a research assistant. He has basic financial literacy knowledge but is struggling to save money and maintain a budget. Jake is looking for resources that can provide personalized budgeting tools and tips on how to save money effectively. He is a busy person and appreciates resources that are concise and easy to use.

Goals

  • Develop effective saving habits.
  • Improve budgeting skill.
  • Give his wife the best life has to offer.

Frustrations

  • Struggling to save money and maintain a budget.
  • Difficulty finding resources that are personalized and effective.
  • Limited time to devote to financial literacy due to work and other responsibilities.

Emily Kolawole.

Occupation
Office Secretary
Location
Lagos, Nigeria
Age
24
Family
Lives with parents

Problem Statement

Emily is a recent graduate with student loan debt who needs a comprehensive financial literacy education platform because she wants to manage her finances effectively but finds it challenging to navigate the complex financial landscape on her own.

Biography

Emily is a university graduate who recently started her first job. She has limited knowledge of financial literacy and is struggling to manage her finances. She is feeling overwhelmed by the amount of information available and is in search of a resource that can provide her with a comprehensive understanding of financial literacy in a way that is easy to understand. Emily is a visual learner and appreciates resources that incorporate interactive elements and visuals.

Goals

  • Have enough money to go on a vacation outside the country.
  • Live a comfortable life free from worries about money.
  • Give his wife the best life has to offer.

Frustrations

  • Overwhelmed by the amount of unstructured financial information available online.
  • “Every month I try to create a budget because it seems like my salary disappears quickly, but I always fail.”
  • Used ineffective financial literacy resources in the past.
